A pump is a mechanical device used to move fluid. Its typical application in a central plant is to move chilled or hot water and condenser water through the loop. Booster or secondary pumps are sometimes used in individual buildings to supplement the pump at the central plant. The most commonly used pump for central plant applications is the centrifugal pump.

Examples of pumps in HVAC system Photos by Dennis Schroeder/NREL 38460, 38461

Pumps are broadly classified as end suction and inline pumps. For further classification of both these pump types, refer to the Pump Handbook from Grundfos Industry.
