Thermal energy storage is a method of storing heating or cooling thermal energy by running equipment at off-peak hours. Ice, water, and phase change material are some commonly used storage media. Hot storage systems are most suitable for applications in which:

  • Peak total heating load of the facility is significantly higher than the average load
  • Utility's electric rate structure has high demand charges, ratchet charges, or a significant difference between on-peak and off-peak rates
  • Facility is considering expanding heating capacity
  • An existing storage tank is available
  • Redundancy in heating systems is needed to make up for short-term heating requirement.

Refer to Thermal Energy Storage Overview from the U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy.
