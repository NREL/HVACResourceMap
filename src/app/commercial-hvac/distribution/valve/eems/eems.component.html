Valve Sizing Considerations

The Chilled Water Plant Design Guide has a detailed section on valve selection criteria. Rules of thumb play a significant role in valve design. One example of a rule of thumb is that if a valve is sized to operate between 60% and 80% open at maximum flow and not less than 20% open at minimum flow, it yields the best flow control result (Source: Valin).

Few important valve sizing considerations from the Chilled Water Plant Design Guide are listed below. Refer to section 5, pages 14–17 for more details.

  • Determine valve pressure drop such that it is a significant portion of the overall system pressure drop. Oversized valves will cause large flow variations with small change in valve position, while an undersized valve will introduce a large pressure drop.
  • Select valve based on pressure drop and valve flow coefficient (Cv).
  • Estimate number of actuation cycles each valve is likely to be subjected to in a year.
  • Clearly specify operational environment and performance requirements of each valve.
  • Check for close-off pressure capability, especially for gate valves.
  • Wherever possible, purchase control valve and actuator as one unit.

Valve Insulation

Prevention of heat loss through valves is important on steam, hot water, and cold water lines. Valves are also insulated as a safety measure on steam lines. Refer to the U.S. Department of Energy Tip Sheet #17: Installing Removable Insulation on Valves and Fittings. The document includes a table that gives energy savings in Btu/hr for different valve sizes across operating temperatures ranging from 200°–600°F.
